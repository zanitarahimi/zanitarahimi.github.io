Here's a time line of Mother Teresa's life:

  • 1910 - born in Skopje, modern Republic of Macedonia
  • 1919 - died her father Nikolle Bojaxhiu
  • 1922 - she commited herself to a religious life
  • 1928 - Agnes left home to join the Sisters of Loreto
  • 1929 - she arrived in India and began her novitiate in Darjeeling, near the Himalayan mountains.
  • 1931 - she took her first religious vows as a nun
  • 1937 - she took her solemn vowa
  • 1944 - Teresa was appointed as headmistress
  • 1948 - she began her missionary work with the poor
  • 1949 - she was joined in her effort by a group of young women and laid the foundations of a new religious community helping the "poorest among the poor"
  • 1950 - Teresa received Vatican permission to start the diocesan congregation that would become the Missionaries of Charity
  • 1952 - she opened the first Home for the Dying in space made available by the city of Calcutta
  • 1955 - she opened the Nirmala Shishu Bhavan , as a haven for orphans and homeless youth
  • 1963 - the Missionaries of Charity Brothers was founded
  • 1965 - was opened the first house outside India , in Venezuela with five sisters
  • 1970 - the congregation opened houses and foundations in dozens of countries in Asia, Africa, Europe and the United States
  • 1976 - was founded a contemplative branch of the Sisters
  • 1981 - Mother Teresa began the Corpus Christi Movement for Priests
  • 1982 - she rescued 37 children trapped in a front line hospital
  • 1984 - Mother Teresan and Fr. Joseph Langford founded the Missionaries of Charity Fathers
  • 1988 - she visited the Soviet republic of Armenia and met with Nikolai Ryzhkov
  • 1989 - after a second attack , she received an artificial pacemaker
  • 1991 - after having pneumonia while in Mexico , she suffered further heart problems
  • 1991 - Mother Teresa returned for the first time to her homeland and opened a Missionaries of Charity Brothers home in Tirana,Albania
  • 1991 - in April , she fell and broke her collar bone
  • 1996 - she was operating 517 missions in more than 100 countries
  • 1997 - on 13 March she stepped down from the head of Missionaries of Charity
  • 1997 - she died on 5 September 1997.

She is best known for her sayings :

The most terrible poverty is loneliness and the feeling of being unloved.

If you judge people, you have no time to love them.

There are no great things, only small thing with great love. Happy are those.

Spread love everywhere you go. Let no one ever come to you without leaving happier.

If you can't feed a hundred people, then feed just one.
